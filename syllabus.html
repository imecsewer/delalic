Course Outcomes

As a result of this class students will know, understand and be able to do the following:

  1. Differentiate the relationship between functions and relations and extend strategies for solving equations and inequalities.
  2. Investigate and solve linear systems.
  3. Investigate and solve quadratic, polynomial, rational, radical, exponential, and logarithmic functions.
  4. Investigate rational exponents, the complex number system and Fundamental Theorem of Algebra.
  5. Investigate understanding of periodic functions and trigonometry.


Course Description

This curriculum is built on a foundation of problem solving and visual learning, aligned to the Common Core State Standards for advanced algebra. The topics above will be covered by interactive media, group problem solving, classroom discussion, and teacher and/or student presentations. Students will build on previous learning about linear and quadratic functions. Additionally, students learn to solve problems modeled by exponential and logarithmic functions, systems of equations and inequalities, and inverse variation. Students extend their understanding of the number systems to include complex numbers. Students will be introduced to periodic functions and basic trigonometric functions.
